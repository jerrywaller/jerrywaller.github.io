Although I gained my SAS experience through biostatistics and clinical trials analysis, I found that SAS is exceptionally useful for a variety of tasks... even in a library.

The Problem: records kept in the library's catalog, Millennium, have no consistent standard for data entry. This makes analyzing the data difficult, and Millenium's built–in reporting mechanisms leave a lot to be desired. Millennium contains a ton of useful information, but getting it into a useable form can seem daunting.

The Solution: In addition to being a great statistical analysis tool, SAS is also really good at cleaning data. Using SAS I scripted a program to take the dirty Millennium data and clean it up. To view a part of the program, please visit my page at the SAS Community wiki here:

Cleaning Library Catalog Data

This program gives a good idea as to how I like my code to look, as well as work. I am a big believer in commented code. Not only does it tell me at a glance what my code is doing, it assists anyone reviewing and evaluating it.
